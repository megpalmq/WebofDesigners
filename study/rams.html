About Dieter Rams

Dieter Rams is one of the most influential industrial designers of the 20th century, best known for his work at Braun and his “Less, but better” philosophy. His design principles — rooted in clarity, simplicity, and honesty — have profoundly shaped modern product design and continue to inspire generations of designers and brands, including Apple.

Ten Principles of Good Design

Good design is innovative.
Good design makes a product useful.
Good design is aesthetic.
Good design makes a product understandable.
Good design is unobtrusive.
Good design is honest.
Good design is long-lasting.
Good design is thorough down to the last detail.
Good design is environmentally friendly.
Good design is as little design as possible.

Legacy

Rams’ disciplined design thinking paved the way for modern digital and physical product aesthetics. His timeless approach to form and function can be seen in today’s minimalist interfaces and devices, proving that good design endures through restraint, purpose, and clarity.
