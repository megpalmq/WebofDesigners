MASSIMO & LELLA VIGNELLI

Modernists | Architects | System Thinkers

Design Is One

Massimo and Lella Vignelli defined an era of modernism built on discipline, clarity, and timelessness. Their work — from the New York City Subway Map to the American Airlines identity — was guided by a singular philosophy: that design should bring order to complexity.

They believed in the power of the grid, in Helvetica as a tool of clarity, and in the idea that good design lasts forever. Their motto, “Design is One,” reminds us that whether you design a chair, a book, or a city system — the process is the same.

The Vignelli Canon

Discipline

The Grid

Legacy

The Vignellis’ influence is still seen today in every clean typographic layout and systematic design system. They reminded us that design is not decoration — it’s communication. Their impact continues to shape corporate identity, editorial design, and urban information systems across the world.

“If you can design one thing, you can design everything.” — Massimo Vignelli
