About Paula Scher

Paula Scher is one of the most influential graphic designers of our time. Known for her expressive use of typography and energetic compositions, Scher’s work bridges the gap between fine art and graphic communication. From album covers in the 1970s to her iconic identity systems for brands like Citibank, the Public Theater, and Microsoft, Scher has redefined what design can say — and how loudly it can say it.

Design Approach

Scher’s designs often scream rather than whisper. They use scale, color, and typographic rhythm to make words the heroes of the composition. Her approach is both chaotic and controlled — a visual dance between order and rebellion.

Legacy

Paula Scher continues to influence generations of designers by proving that typography can be both a visual and emotional experience. Her bold compositions remind us that design isn’t just about communication — it’s about expression, impact, and cultural voice.

“Words have meaning and type has spirit.” — Paula Scher
