Designers · Architects · Filmmakers · Explorers

About Charles & Ray Eames

Charles and Ray Eames were a revolutionary design team whose work spanned architecture, industrial design, furniture, graphic design, and film. They believed in the idea that design was **a way of life**, constantly solving problems to make the best for the most for the least. Their meticulous process and playful curiosity led to some of the 20th century's most enduring and democratic designs.

Core Design Principles

Function & Form

A commitment to solving problems first. Their work is an elegant synthesis of utility and beautiful shape.

Molded Plywood

Pioneering techniques to bend plywood in complex curves, creating lightweight, strong, and affordable furniture.

"Tension of Two"

Their partnership was a dynamic balance. Charles was the structure, and Ray was the texture, color, and art.

Iconic Works

Legacy

The Eameses' impact extends far beyond furniture; they set the standard for mid-century modern design and taught the world that **play and precision** can co-exist. Their belief in continuous exploration and iterative problem-solving remains a cornerstone of modern design thinking.

“The details are not the details. They make the product.” — Charles Eames
